Computer Cooling Systems

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.2

Airflow Basics

  • Heat is generated by all internal computer components.
  • Cooling is achieved by pulling cool air through the case using fans.
  • Standard airflow design:
    • Cool air enters one side of the case.
    • Air passes over warm components (absorbing heat).
    • Hot air is expelled from the opposite side.
  • Critical factors for optimal airflow:
    • Component layout on the motherboard.
    • Cable management (keep wires organized).
    • Unobstructed path from intake to exhaust.

Fan-Based Cooling

  • Fans are the primary active cooling method.
  • Common fan sizes:
    • 80mm (smaller, louder).
    • 120mm (standard, balanced).
    • 200mm (larger, quieter).
  • Variable-speed fans:
    • Run slower when system is cool.
    • Speed increases as temperature rises.
    • Noise levels correlate with speed (faster = louder).
  • Specialized fans:
    • High-end graphics cards often include onboard fans.
    • Requires adequate physical space (common on larger cards).

Passive Cooling

  • Fanless cooling for noise-sensitive environments.
  • Common applications:
    • Video servers.
    • Set-top boxes.
    • Purpose-built appliances.
  • Relies on:
    • Heat sinks (spreads heat over a larger area).
    • Natural convection (no moving parts).
  • Limitations:
    • Only effective for low-heat systems.
    • Requires adequate airflow in the environment.

Heat Sinks

  • Function: Dissipates heat from components.
  • Design:
    • Multiple fins increase surface area.
    • Heat transfers from component → fins → air.
  • Safety considerations:
    • Heat sinks get extremely hot during operation.
    • Risk of burns if touched after use.
  • Thermal interface materials (TIM):
    • Thermal paste (thermal grease):
      • Fills microscopic gaps between component and heat sink.
      • Application: pea-sized amount (spreads on contact).
      • Non-reusable (replace if heat sink is removed).
    • Thermal pads:
      • Pre-cut, mess-free alternative to paste.
      • Less effective than paste but safer (no leakage risk).
      • Non-reusable (replace if removed).

Liquid Cooling

  • Used for high-performance systems:
    • Gaming PCs.
    • Overclocked systems.
    • High-end workstations.
  • Components:
    • Water block (replaces traditional heat sink).
    • Radiator (dissipates heat via fans).
    • Pipes (circulate coolant).
    • Pump (maintains fluid movement).
  • Advantages:
    • Superior cooling for extreme heat loads.
    • Can be quieter than air cooling (fewer/faster fans).
  • Considerations:
    • More complex installation.
    • Risk of leaks (though rare in modern systems).
    • Higher cost than air cooling.

Cooling System Selection

  • Factors to consider:
    • Heat output of components (CPU, GPU).
    • Case size (affects fan/radiator compatibility).
    • Noise tolerance (passive < liquid < air).
    • Budget (passive < air < liquid).
    • Overclocking (liquid recommended).
  • Hybrid approaches:
    • Combine air cooling (case fans) with liquid cooling (CPU).
    • Use heat sinks + fans for GPUs.
