Dot Matrix Printers

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 4.1

Overview

  • Impact printer that creates output using a matrix of pins pressing against an ink ribbon.
  • Characters are composed of tiny dots formed by the pins.
  • Commonly used for carbon copies or multi-part forms due to its impact mechanism.
  • Low cost per page but noisy and produces low-resolution output.

Components & Operation

  • Print Head:
    • Contains a matrix of pins (typically 9, 18, or 24 pins).
    • Moves back and forth across the page.
    • Pins strike the ribbon to transfer ink to paper.
    • Requires a heat sink due to heat generation.
  • Ribbon:
    • Single, continuous ink ribbon housed in a replaceable cartridge.
    • Stretches across the page width for seamless printing.
    • Easy to replace (takes seconds).
    • Model-specific; ensure compatibility when replacing.
  • Paper Handling:
    • Uses tractor feed (holes on paper sides align with printer sprockets).
    • Paper types:
      • Continuous paper (perforated or unperforated).
      • Green bar paper (used for source code output).
      • Multi-part paper (carbonless copies via microencapsulated ink + clay reaction).
    • Misalignment causes paper jams.

Advantages & Disadvantages

  • Advantages:
    • Low cost per page for high-volume printing.
    • Ideal for multi-part forms (e.g., invoices, receipts).
    • Durable in harsh environments (e.g., workshops, warehouses).
    • Compatible with carbonless copy paper.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Noisy (unsuitable for quiet environments like libraries).
    • Low resolution (poor for graphics or high-quality text).
    • Slow compared to modern printers.
    • Skin irritation risk from multi-part paper chemicals.
    • Declining support; niche use cases only.

Use Cases

  • Niche Applications:
    • Printing multi-part forms (e.g., invoices, shipping labels).
    • Legacy systems requiring impact printing (e.g., ATMs, industrial machines).
    • Carbon copies for legal/financial records.
    • Programming output (e.g., green bar paper for source code).
  • Decline in Popularity:
    • Replaced by laser and inkjet printers for most use cases.
    • Modern alternatives offer better speed, quality, and quiet operation.

Maintenance & Troubleshooting

  • Ribbon Replacement:
    • Pop out old cartridge and insert new one (model-specific).
    • Ensure ribbon is properly seated to avoid print quality issues.
  • Paper Jams:
    • Check tractor feed alignment.
    • Ensure holes on paper match printer sprockets.
    • Avoid forcing misaligned paper.
  • Print Quality Issues:
    • Clean print head pins if dots are missing.
    • Replace ribbon if faded or streaky output.
