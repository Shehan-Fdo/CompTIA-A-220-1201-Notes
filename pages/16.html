Internet Connection Types

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.2

Satellite Networking

  • Provides internet access globally, even in remote areas.
  • Works by communicating from Earth → satellite → Earth.
  • Cost: More expensive than terrestrial options but functional for remote sites.
  • Speeds: Typically 100 Mbps down / 5 Mbps up.
  • Latency:
    • Traditional: ~500ms (250ms up/down).
    • Starlink: 25–60ms (lower due to closer orbit).
  • Limitations:
    • Requires line of sight to satellite.
    • Rainfade: Connectivity issues during storms.

Fiber Optic

  • Uses light to transmit data through thin glass fibers.
  • Advantages:
    • High bandwidth (efficient for large data).
    • Long-distance transmission (ideal for WANs).
    • Supports multi-wavelength connections (e.g., SONET rings).
  • Disadvantages:
    • Higher cost (fiber + equipment).
    • Expensive repairs vs. copper.
  • Deployment:
    • Traditionally for WANs/corporate cores.
    • Now available for home use (converted to copper indoors).

Copper-Based Connections

  • Cable (Broadband):
    • Uses coaxial cable (shared with TV).
    • Technology: DOCSIS (Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification).
    • Speeds: 50 Mbps–1 Gbps+.
    • Supports voice, video, data simultaneously via frequency division.
  • DSL (Digital Subscriber Line):
    • Uses phone lines for digital data.
    • Types: ADSL (Asymmetric DSL—faster download than upload).
    • Speeds: e.g., 200 Mbps down / 20 Mbps up.
    • Limitations:
      • Speed degrades with distance from central office.
      • Max range: ~10,000 feet.

Cellular Networking

  • Uses mobile phone infrastructure (cells + antennas).
  • Tethering:
    • One-to-one device connection via phone.
  • Mobile Hotspot:
    • Shares phone’s internet with multiple devices.
    • May incur additional costs (check carrier).

Wireless ISP (WISP)

  • Ideal for remote areas without copper/fiber access.
  • Setup: Requires an outdoor antenna.
  • Technologies:
    • 802.11 mesh (Wi-Fi-like).
    • 5G home internet (via mobile providers).
    • Proprietary wireless solutions.
  • Speeds: 10 Mbps–1 Gbps.
