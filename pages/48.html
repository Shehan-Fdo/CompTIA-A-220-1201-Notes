Inkjet Printer Maintenance

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 4.3

Print Head Maintenance

  • Cleaning processes vary by manufacturer; always check best practices.
  • Most printers run an automated cleaning cycle every 24 hours to remove dried/excess ink.
  • Manual cleaning may be required for heavy usage (streaks indicate excess ink).
  • Advanced users can remove and manually clean the print head (requires caution).
  • Example: Before/after cleaning shows significant ink accumulation over 24 hours.

Ink Cartridges

  • Color models: CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black).
  • Configurations:
    • Combined cartridges (e.g., CMY in one, K separate).
    • Modular cartridges (individual CMYK for easy replacement).
  • Replacement process:
    • Cartridges pop in/out of holders (takes seconds).
    • Always recycle plastic cartridges.

Calibration

  • Runs automatically after cartridge replacement (aligns colors).
  • Manual calibration may improve output crispness.
  • Calibration page includes:
    • Color blocks (CMYK samples).
    • Alignment marks for precise adjustments.

Paper Jams

  • Causes: Paper caught in mechanisms/path.
  • Resolution steps:
    • Open printer cover for full path access.
    • Remove paper gently to avoid tearing (check for leftover fragments).
    • Verify path is clear before resuming printing.

End of Notes
