SATA Interface Overview

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 2.1

Introduction to SATA

  • SATA stands for Serial AT Attachment, a standard interface for connecting hard drives inside desktop computers.
  • Designed as a one-to-one connection between the drive and motherboard (no daisy-chaining).
  • Supports both internal (SATA) and external (eSATA) connections.

SATA Versions and Speeds

  • Evolution of SATA revisions and their speeds:
    • SATA 1.0: 1.5 Gbps (150 MB/s).
    • SATA 2.0: 3 Gbps (300 MB/s).
    • SATA 3.0: 6 Gbps (600 MB/s).
    • SATA 3.2: 16 Gbps (via SATA Express).
  • Each revision doubles the speed of its predecessor (except 3.2).

Physical Connectors

  • Two distinct connectors on a SATA drive:
    • Data connector: 7-pin L-shaped port for data transfer.
    • Power connector: 15-pin wider port for power delivery.
  • Some drives also support legacy Molex power connectors.
  • Cables are dedicated:
    • One SATA power cable per drive (from PSU).
    • One SATA data cable per drive (to motherboard).

Motherboard Connections

  • SATA ports on motherboards are typically:
    • Grouped near the bottom edge.
    • Color-coded (e.g., blue for SATA 2, black/orange for SATA 3).
    • Labeled (e.g., SATA0, SATA1).
  • Number of ports = maximum supported drives (e.g., 6 ports = 6 drives).
  • No daisy-chaining: Each drive requires its own data and power cable.

eSATA (External SATA)

  • Purpose: Connects external drives via a cable (up to 2 meters).
  • Key differences from SATA:
    • Different connector shape (incompatible with internal SATA).
    • Same signal protocol as internal SATA.
    • Requires a dedicated eSATA cable and port (usually on the PC’s rear I/O panel).

Connection Process

  • Steps to connect a SATA drive:
    1. Align and plug the 7-pin data cable into the drive and motherboard.
    2. Connect the 15-pin power cable from the PSU to the drive.
    3. For eSATA, use the external port and a compatible cable.
  • Troubleshooting tip: Ensure cables are fully seated (audible click).

End of Notes
