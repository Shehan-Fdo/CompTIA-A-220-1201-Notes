Ethernet Wiring Standards (T568A & T568B)

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 2.2

International Ethernet Standards

  • Standardization managed by ISO/IEC 11801 (international) and ANSI/TIA-568 (US).
  • TIA (Telecommunications Industry Association) defines cabling standards for commercial buildings.
  • Standards ensure consistency in wiring across installations and troubleshooting.

T568A vs. T568B Color Schemes

  • Both use RJ45 connectors but differ in wire-color pin assignments.
  • T568B is more common in the US and globally.
  • Organizations typically stick to one standard for consistency.
  • Never mix T568A and T568B on the same cable (e.g., one end A, other end B).
  • Myth: Mixing A/B is not a crossover cable—it causes connectivity issues.

Pinout Comparisons

  • T568A: 1: White/Green, 2: Green, 3: White/Orange, 4: Blue, 5: White/Blue, 6: Orange, 7: White/Brown, 8: Brown.
  • T568B: 1: White/Orange, 2: Orange, 3: White/Green, 4: Blue, 5: White/Blue, 6: Green, 7: White/Brown, 8: Brown.
  • Similarities: Pins 4/5 (Blue) and 7/8 (Brown) are identical in both standards.
  • Only pins 1/2 and 3/6 differ—memorize these variations.

Practical Applications

  • Critical for crimping and cable management in networking.
  • Verify wire alignment in RJ45 connectors before crimping.
  • Keystone jacks may label A/B schemes for easier installation.
  • Some jacks use custom color guides but internally map to T568A/B.
  • Example: A jack might split wires into two groups of four, with A/B labels for each.

Troubleshooting Tips

  • Inspect cable ends to identify the standard used (e.g., White/Orange on pin 1 = T568B).
  • Use a cable tester to verify continuity and correct pinout.
  • Re-crimp if wires are misaligned or damaged.
