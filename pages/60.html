Mobile Device Troubleshooting

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 2.4

Battery Issues

  • Batteries degrade over time and may need replacement if they fail to hold a charge.
  • Rapid battery drain can be caused by:
    • Poor signal reception (constant signal searching).
    • Unused features like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or GPS.
  • Enable Airplane Mode in low-signal areas to conserve battery.
  • Check battery health in Settings > Battery (iOS, iPadOS, Android) for usage stats and health status.
  • Swollen batteries are hazardous:
    • Indicate gas buildup and potential fire risk.
    • Can distort or damage the device.
    • Replace immediately—do not puncture or continue use.

Display and Glass Damage

  • Broken glass often requires full display replacement (glass is typically integrated).
  • Back up data before attempting repairs.
  • Handle broken glass carefully:
    • Use screen protectors or glass tape to cover sharp edges.
    • Avoid direct contact to prevent cuts.

Charging Problems

  • Check all charging components:
    • Cable: Test with a known-working cable.
    • Interface: Inspect for debris or damage.
    • Power adapter: Use a multimeter to verify output.
    • Wall outlet: Ensure it’s functional.
  • Intermittent charging may indicate interface damage, requiring system board replacement.

Connectivity Issues

  • Poor cellular signal:
    • Check proximity to towers.
    • Step outside if indoors for better reception.
  • Wi-Fi issues:
    • Ensure proximity to the access point.
    • Change channels/frequencies in congested areas.
    • Avoid interference from other devices.

Liquid Damage

  • Liquid Contact Indicators (LCI) change color when exposed to moisture (located in charging/SIM ports).
  • Immediate steps if exposed to liquid:
    • Power off and do not charge.
    • Remove case, batteries, and cards.
    • Dry with desiccants (not rice) for 24+ hours.
    • Avoid heat, movement, or powering on prematurely.
  • If the device powers on after drying, test functionality; otherwise, seek professional repair.

Overheating

  • Causes:
    • Direct sunlight or high ambient temperatures.
    • CPU-intensive apps or charging while in use.
  • Mitigation:
    • Avoid leaving devices in hot environments (e.g., car dashboards).
    • Close unused apps to reduce CPU load.
    • Check battery usage stats to identify power-hungry apps.
  • Devices may auto-shutoff to prevent damage.

Unresponsive Devices

  • Reset procedures vary by device:
    • Apple: Hold Power + Home/Volume for 10 seconds.
    • Android: Varies by manufacturer (e.g., Power + Volume combinations or battery removal).
  • Consult device documentation for exact steps.

Physical Damage

  • Damaged interfaces (e.g., USB) may require system board replacement.
  • Symptoms:
    • Intermittent or failed charging.
    • Data transfer issues.
  • Inspect interfaces for visible damage or debris.

Malware and Security

  • Signs of infection:
    • Unusual apps or high data usage.
    • Excessive CPU/memory usage by unknown apps.
  • Solutions:
    • Run anti-malware/anti-virus software.
    • Review app permissions and installed apps.

Cursor Drift

  • Symptoms: Screen interacts without touch input.
  • Causes:
    • Faulty digitizer or display.
    • Miscalibration (older devices may have calibration apps).
  • Solutions:
    • Recalibrate if possible.
    • Replace display if hardware-related.

App Installation Issues

  • Common causes:
    • Insufficient storage space.
    • Poor network connectivity (large apps require stable bandwidth).
    • Incompatible app/OS version.
    • Missing authentication credentials.
  • Check:
    • Available storage and clear unnecessary data.
    • App compatibility with device/OS.
    • Network stability for downloads.

Stylus Problems

  • Troubleshooting steps:
    • Check battery level (active styluses require power).
    • Verify Bluetooth pairing.
    • Inspect for physical damage (e.g., broken tip).
    • Replace tips if worn.
    • Reset stylus and device if issues persist.

Performance Issues

  • Causes:
    • Outdated OS/apps.
    • Insufficient storage (slowdowns or crashes).
    • Faulty hardware (e.g., damaged storage).
    • Background apps consuming resources.
    • Outdated device unable to run modern apps.
  • Solutions:
    • Update OS and apps.
    • Free up storage space.
    • Close unused background apps.
    • Upgrade device if hardware is obsolete.
