Network Cabling Fundamentals

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 2.1

Introduction to Network Cabling

  • Network cabling is the foundation of all data transmission, including wired and wireless networks.
  • Poor cabling leads to performance issues or complete failure of network applications.
  • Proper planning and installation are critical for reliable network operation.
  • Even wireless networks rely on physical cabling (e.g., access points connected via Ethernet).

Twisted Pair Copper Cables

  • Most common type of networking cable for Ethernet.
  • Called twisted pair because wires are twisted in pairs to reduce interference.
  • Each pair carries equal and opposite signals (transmit+ and transmit-).
  • Twisting cancels out electromagnetic interference (EMI) via differential signaling.
  • Each pair has a different twist rate to minimize crosstalk.
  • Standards define minimum capabilities for each cable category (e.g., bandwidth, speed).
  • Speed depends on the signaling method, not the cable itself.

Cable Categories and Standards

  • Standards are defined by IEEE 802.3 for Ethernet.
  • Minimum cable category required for each Ethernet standard:
    • 1000BASE-T (Gigabit Ethernet): Cat 5 (deprecated) or Cat 5e (enhanced, supports 100m).
    • 10GBASE-T (10G Ethernet):
      • Cat 6 (UTP): 55m max.
      • Cat 6 (STP): 100m max.
      • Cat 6a (Augmented): 100m max.
  • Cables are labeled with their category (e.g., Cat 5e, Cat 6a).
  • Always refer to IEEE standards for minimum cable requirements.

Coaxial Cables

  • Used for high-speed internet (e.g., cable modems, digital cable).
  • Structure:
    • Inner conductor: Carries the signal.
    • Outer shield: Protects against interference.
  • Common applications:
    • Broadband internet.
    • CCTV systems.
    • Legacy Ethernet (e.g., 10BASE2, 10BASE5).

Shielding Types

  • Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP):
    • No shielding around pairs or overall cable.
    • Most common for general use.
  • Shielded Twisted Pair (STP):
    • Shielding can be:
      • Foil (F).
      • Braided (S).
    • Applied to individual pairs or entire cable.
  • Cable labeling format: [Overall Shield]/[Pair Shield]TP.
    • Examples:
      • S/FTP: Braided overall, foil per pair.
      • F/UTP: Foil overall, no pair shielding.
      • U/UTP: No shielding (standard UTP).

Specialized Cabling

  • Direct Burial Cables:
    • Designed for underground installation.
    • Features:
      • Waterproof (gel-filled or sealed).
      • Shielded (STP) to protect against interference.
      • Drain wire for grounding.
      • Structurally reinforced for durability.
  • Plenum-Rated Cables:
    • Required for above drop ceilings or air handling spaces.
    • Made with fire-retardant materials:
      • Fluorinated Ethylene Polymer (FEP).
      • Low-Smoke PVC.
    • Prevents spread of fire, smoke, and toxic fumes.
    • Less flexible than standard cables.
