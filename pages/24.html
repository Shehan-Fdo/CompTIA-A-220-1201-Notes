USB, Serial, and Thunderbolt Connections

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.2

Universal Serial Bus (USB) Overview

  • USB (Universal Serial Bus) is the standard connection for peripherals (keyboards, mice, etc.).
  • Evolved through multiple versions with increasing throughput and varying cable lengths.

USB Versions and Specifications

  • USB 1.1:
    • Low Speed: 1.5 Mbps, max 3m cable.
    • Full Speed: 12 Mbps, max 5m cable.
  • USB 2.0:
    • 480 Mbps, max 5m cable.
  • USB 3.0 (SuperSpeed):
    • 5 Gbps, max 3m cable.
  • USB 3.1: 10 Gbps.
  • USB 3.2: 20 Gbps.
  • Cable lengths are approximate; longer cables may reduce throughput.

USB Connectors

  • USB 1.1/2.0 Connectors:
    • Standard-A: Rectangular, used for host devices.
    • Standard-B: Square, used for peripherals (e.g., printers).
    • Mini-B and Micro-B: Smaller connectors for mobile devices.
  • USB 3.0+ Connectors:
    • Standard-A remains similar but with additional pins.
    • Standard-B and Micro-B are taller/wider to accommodate higher speeds.
  • USB-C:
    • Reversible, universal connector replacing all previous types.
    • Similar in size to Micro-B but supports higher data rates and power delivery.
    • Does not define the signal type; can carry USB, Thunderbolt, DisplayPort, etc.

Legacy Serial Connections

  • Pre-USB standard for peripherals (modems, keyboards, mice).
  • RS-232 (Recommended Standard 232): Serial communication standard since 1969.
  • Connectors:
    • DB9 (DE9): 9-pin connector (misnamed; technically E-size).
    • DB25: 25-pin connector (B-size).
  • Modern Use:
    • Legacy equipment (older routers/switches) via console ports.
    • Requires USB-to-DB9 adapters for modern devices.
    • Console ports may use RJ45 (serial, not Ethernet) or USB.
  • Purpose: Out-of-band management when network interfaces fail.

Thunderbolt

  • High-speed serial connection combining data and power.
  • Thunderbolt 1/2:
    • Used Mini DisplayPort connector.
    • Thunderbolt 1: 2 channels × 10 Gbps (20 Gbps total).
    • Thunderbolt 2: 20 Gbps aggregated.
  • Thunderbolt 3/4:
    • Uses USB-C connector.
    • Thunderbolt 3: 40 Gbps, max 3m (copper) or 60m (optical).
    • Supports daisy-chaining up to 6 devices.
    • Thunderbolt 4: 40 Gbps, dual 4K displays, increased PCIe bandwidth.
