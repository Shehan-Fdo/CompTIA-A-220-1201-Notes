Wireless Technologies

Wi-Fi Standards and Naming

  • 802.11 is the IEEE standard for wireless networks, managed by the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE).
  • Old naming convention used 802.11 + letters (e.g., 802.11ac, 802.11ax).
  • New naming convention simplifies versions to Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac), Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax), Wi-Fi 6E (extended 6GHz), and Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be).

Wi-Fi Frequencies and Channels

  • Wi-Fi operates in three primary frequency ranges:
    • 2.4 GHz: Limited channels (3 non-overlapping 20MHz channels), prone to interference.
    • 5 GHz: More channels, supports wider bandwidths (20/40/80/160MHz), less interference.
    • 6 GHz: Introduced with Wi-Fi 6E, offers even more spectrum and reduced congestion.
  • Channels group frequencies for easier reference (e.g., Channel 6 = 2.437 GHz, Channel 44 = 5.220 GHz).
  • Bandwidth options: 20MHz (standard), 40MHz, 80MHz, or 160MHz (higher bandwidth = higher throughput but fewer available channels).

Other Wireless Technologies

  • Bluetooth:
    • Uses 2.4 GHz ISM band (unlicensed Industrial, Scientific, Medical frequencies).
    • Short range (~10 meters), ideal for personal devices (headsets, speakers, keyboards).
  • RFID (Radio Frequency Identification):
    • Used in access badges, product tracking, pet microchips.
    • Two types:
      • Passive RFID: No battery; powered by scanner’s radio waves (short range).
      • Active RFID: Battery-powered (longer range).
    • Contains an antenna and a chip storing a unique ID code.
  • NFC (Near Field Communication):
    • Extends RFID for two-way communication (vs. RFID’s one-way).
    • Used in:
      • Contactless payments (phones/smartwatches at POS terminals).
      • Device pairing (e.g., Bluetooth/Wi-Fi setup via NFC tap).
      • Access control (e.g., door unlocking via phone).

Key Comparisons

  • Range:
    • Wi-Fi: Building-wide coverage.
    • Bluetooth: ~10 meters (personal area).
    • NFC: Centimeters (tap-to-connect).
    • RFID: Varies (passive = short, active = longer).
  • Frequency Bands:
    • Wi-Fi/Bluetooth: 2.4 GHz (crowded), 5 GHz, 6 GHz (Wi-Fi 6E).
    • RFID/NFC: Typically 13.56 MHz (NFC) or various bands for RFID.
  • Use Cases:
    • Wi-Fi: Internet/network access.
    • Bluetooth: Personal device connectivity.
    • RFID: Identification/tracking.
    • NFC: Secure short-range transactions/data exchange.
