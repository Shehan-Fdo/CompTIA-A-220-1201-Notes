Video Connectors Overview

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.2

HDMI (High Definition Multimedia Interface)

  • Transmits both audio and video over a single digital connection.
  • Max range: ~20 meters before signal degradation (blockiness).
  • Connector type: 19-pin Type A (rectangular with indented bottom corners).
  • Most common on modern monitors and TVs.
  • Example: Found on adapters alongside DVI and DisplayPort.

DisplayPort

  • Sends audio and video in packetized form (similar to Ethernet).
  • Supports passive adapters for HDMI/DVI compatibility.
  • Connector variants:
    • Full-size: Common on desktops/video cards.
    • Mini DisplayPort: Found on some devices.
  • Features:
    • Locking mechanism (prevents accidental disconnection).
    • Unlock via top/side button before removal.
  • Shape: Rectangle with one slanted corner (prevents misplugging with HDMI).

DVI (Digital Visual Interface)

  • Video-only (no audio).
  • Variants:
    • DVI-A: Analog (VGA-compatible).
    • DVI-D: Digital (Single Link: ~3.7 Gbps; Dual Link: double bandwidth).
    • DVI-I: Integrated (supports both digital/analog).
  • Resolutions:
    • Single Link: Up to 1920×1200 (HD).
    • Dual Link: Higher resolutions.
  • Check connector types on device/cable for compatibility.

VGA (Video Graphics Array)

  • Legacy analog standard (video-only).
  • Connector: DE15 (DB15) (15-pin, D-shaped, typically blue).
  • Signal degrades beyond 5–10 meters.
  • No audio support.

USB-C and Multi-Purpose Connectors

  • Supports multiple signals via one connector:
    • Power (USB PD).
    • Data (USB/serial).
    • Video: DisplayPort, HDMI, Thunderbolt, MHL (mobile).
  • Requires correct cable/adapter for intended signal type.

Comparison Summary

  • Audio + Video: HDMI, DisplayPort, USB-C (with alt modes).
  • Video-Only: DVI, VGA.
  • Digital: HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI-D.
  • Analog: VGA, DVI-A.
  • Locking Connector: DisplayPort.
  • Max Range:
    • HDMI: ~20m.
    • VGA: ~5–10m.
