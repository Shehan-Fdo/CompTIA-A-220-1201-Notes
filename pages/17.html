Network Types Overview

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.2

Local Area Network (LAN)

  • Connects devices in a small geographic area (e.g., building floor, campus).
  • Characterized by high-speed connectivity and low latency.
  • Examples: Ethernet (wired) or 802.11 Wi-Fi (wireless).
  • Speed decreases when connecting to remote locations outside the LAN.

Wide Area Network (WAN)

  • Spans large distances (cities, countries, globally).
  • Typically slower than LAN due to distance and infrastructure complexity.
  • Technologies used:
    • Point-to-point serial connections.
    • MPLS (Multi-Protocol Label Switching).
    • Terrestrial networks (ground-based infrastructure).
    • Non-terrestrial networks (satellite communications).

Personal Area Network (PAN)

  • Connects personal devices in close proximity (e.g., within a few meters).
  • Technologies:
    • Bluetooth (wireless headsets, hands-free car systems).
    • Infrared (older devices).
    • NFC (Near Field Communication for contactless payments).
  • Applications:
    • Health/fitness devices (smartwatches, workout trackers).
    • Media integration (phone to car audio).

Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

  • Covers a city-wide or large campus area.
  • Connects multiple LANs within a geographic region.
  • Traditionally proprietary, now often standardized on Metro Ethernet.
  • May use government-owned fiber (e.g., municipal infrastructure).

Storage Area Network (SAN)

  • High-speed network for block-level storage access.
  • Appears as local storage to connected devices.
  • Optimized for large data transfers (e.g., databases, virtualization).
  • Typically isolated from general network traffic for performance.

Wireless LAN (WLAN)

  • Uses 802.11 standards (Wi-Fi) for wireless connectivity.
  • Allows mobility within a local area (home, office, campus).
  • Scalable with additional access points for larger coverage.
