Copper Connectors Overview

CompTIA A+ 220-1201 - Core 1 | Lesson 2.1

RJ-11 and RJ-14 Connectors

  • RJ-11: 6-position, 2-conductor (6P2C) modular connector.
  • Commonly used for analog telephone and DSL connections.
  • RJ-14: 6-position, 4-conductor variant (often mislabeled as RJ-11).
  • Physically smaller than RJ-45; can accidentally fit into RJ-45 ports.

RJ-45 Connectors

  • 8-position, 8-conductor (8P8C) modular connector.
  • Primarily used for Ethernet but supports other connections (e.g., serial).
  • Larger than RJ-11; cannot fit into RJ-11 ports.
  • Requires verification when plugging in to avoid misconnections.

F-Connectors

  • Used for coaxial cable (e.g., cable TV, cable modems).
  • Features a threaded connection for secure attachment.
  • Follows DOCSIS (Data Over Cable Service Interface Specification) standard.
  • Single copper conductor in the center for signal transmission.

Punch-Down Blocks

  • Used for terminating twisted pair cables in bulk (e.g., telecom rooms).
  • Advantages:
    • Fast installation (wires punched down with a tool).
    • Cost-effective (no individual connectors needed).
  • Metal contacts pierce wire insulation to establish connection.
  • Supports unshielded twisted pair (UTP) cables.

USB Connectors

  • USB 1.1/2.0:
    • Type-A: Standard rectangular plug (host-side).
    • Type-B: Square plug (device-side).
    • Mini-B/Micro-B: Smaller variants for mobile devices.
  • USB 3.0+:
    • Type-A remains similar.
    • Type-B and Micro-B redesigned for higher speeds.
  • USB-C:
    • Reversible, universal connector for USB, Thunderbolt, HDMI, etc.
    • Standardizes power/data delivery across devices.

Molex Connectors

  • 4-pin power connector for internal PC peripherals (fans, drives).
  • Provides 12V and 5V power.
  • Modular, friction-based connection; no tools required.
  • Common in older ATX power supplies.

Lightning Connectors

  • Apple’s proprietary connector for iPhones, iPads.
  • Features:
    • Reversible design.
    • Higher power delivery than Micro-USB.
  • Being phased out in favor of USB-C (industry standard).

DB9 (RS-232) Connectors

  • 9-pin serial connector for legacy devices (routers, switches).
  • Follows RS-232 standard (serial communication).
  • Used for:
    • Console ports on networking hardware.
    • Direct configuration via command line.
  • Predates USB; still relevant for troubleshooting.
