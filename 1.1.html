Laptop Hardware

CompTIA A+ 220-1101 - Core 1 | Lesson 1.1

Hardware Overview

  • Laptops are built to precise specifications, making repairs challenging due to the compact and proprietary nature of their components.
  • The ease of repair varies widely. Some models are designed to be user-serviceable, while others require a complete teardown for simple upgrades.

Laptop Batteries

  • Function: Provides power when the laptop is not connected to an AC outlet.
  • Common Types:
    • Lithium-ion (Li-ion) and Lithium-ion polymer (LiPo) are standard.
    • They do not suffer from the "memory effect," so they can be charged at any time without degrading their long-term capacity.
  • Form Factor & Replacement:
    • Modular: Easily removable and can be replaced in minutes. Common in older or business-class laptops.
    • Integrated: Built into the laptop case. Replacement is difficult and often requires a full device teardown.
    • Each battery is specific to a laptop model; you cannot interchange them.

Keyboard & Keys

  • High Usage: As a primary input device, the keyboard is one of the most frequently replaced components.
  • Replacement: Many laptops allow keyboard replacement by removing a bezel and a few screws, then disconnecting a single ribbon cable. However, some designs are much more integrated.
  • Troubleshooting Tip: If the built-in keyboard fails, connect an external USB keyboard to confirm if the problem is with the hardware or the operating system.
  • Individual Keys: Replacing a single key cap is possible but requires care, as the plastic scissor-switch mechanisms underneath are very delicate and easily broken.

Memory (RAM)

  • Laptops use a compact memory format called SO-DIMM (Small Outline Dual In-line Memory Module).
  • Upgradability:
    • Modular: Most laptops have accessible memory slots, allowing for easy upgrades or replacements.
    • Soldered: Some models, particularly ultra-portables, have the RAM soldered directly to the motherboard. This memory cannot be upgraded, and if it fails, the entire motherboard must be replaced.

Storage Drives

  • Magnetic Disk (HDD): Uses traditional spinning platters. Laptop HDDs use a 2.5-inch form factor.
  • Solid-State Drive (SSD): Uses flash memory with no moving parts. Offers significantly better performance, reliability, and silence.
    • 2.5-inch SSD: Same size as a laptop HDD, serving as a direct, easy replacement.
    • M.2 SSD: A smaller, card-like drive that plugs directly into an M.2 slot on the motherboard, eliminating the need for data and power cables.
  • Migrating from HDD to SSD:
    • Provides a massive performance boost, making an old laptop feel new.
    • This can be done by performing a fresh OS install or by using imaging/cloning software to create an exact copy of the HDD onto the new SSD. Many SSDs come with this software included.

Wireless & Connectivity

  • 802.11 (Wi-Fi) & Bluetooth: Often combined on a single adapter, either integrated into the motherboard or on a separate card.
    • Adapter Cards: Common formats include Mini PCI and Mini PCI Express. These are modular and replaceable.
  • Antennas: The physical antenna wires are routed from the adapter card up and around the laptop screen to achieve the best possible signal strength.
    • Connections are typically labeled MAIN and AUX for Wi-Fi, with a possible third connection for Bluetooth.
  • NFC (Near-field communication): A very short-range (4cm or less) wireless technology used for payments, data transfer, and authentication (e.g., tapping a badge to log in).

Integrated Peripherals

  • Biometrics: Authentication using unique physical traits.
    • Fingerprint Reader: A small scanner on the laptop chassis.
    • Face Recognition: Uses the built-in webcam (e.g., Windows Hello).
    • Both require hardware support on the laptop and configuration within the operating system.
  • Camera/Webcam & Microphone:
    • Almost all modern laptops include a built-in webcam and microphone at the top of the display, essential for video conferencing.
    • While convenient, their quality is often basic. For professional use, an external USB microphone or webcam is recommended.
